About Me

About Me

My table tennis achievements

  • Commonwealth Games 2018 bronze medalist
  • Finishing 2nd in Senior womens ranking before switching nationality to Seychelles
  • Gold in under 11, 12, 13, 15, 18 and 21 Nationals
  • Number 1 in each age catogary from under 11 to under 21's
  • Played in French league N2, Austrian premier league, Bundaslega, European league, Spanish league N2
  • Played for England abroad in individual and team tournaments

Current

    I am currently completing my masters in Physiotherapy at St George's University of London and will graduate in July. I have achieved a First Class in Sport Rehabilitation in my undergraduate degree at Nottingham University. Throughout my undergraduate and master degree, I have moved towards being very pationate about combining both physiotherapy and table tennis to help promote long-term exercise and build a community.

More About Me

I had multiple coaches growing up and learnt many diverse aspects of table tennis through each coach. Luckily at my local club growing up, I had about 5-7 coaches throughout the age of 7-16 putting input, care and support towards my table tennis development. I moved to Austria at the age of 16 alone to train full time in table tennis at Linz AG Froschberg Club for 2.5 years and further developed my game with exposure to diverse top female players. I then moved to attend Nottingham University where I achieved a First Class Honour in Sports Rehabilitation degree and adapted my table tennis strategies. I am currently completing my Master's degree at St George's University of London and have moved back to North London. Due to Table Tennis England changing their system and not having the funding to support full time practice with the England team, in 2023 I decided to change my table tennis representing nationality to Seychelles. I went for the first time to visit my family in Seychlles and went to a training camp over 2023 summer. I really connected with the culture, people and atmosphere. I felt seen and valued in my table tennis and excited to represent them in future evenets and therefore felt this was the right desicion. So therefore, I have to wait 5 years until I can represent my country in bigger events. My dream is to see how far I can make it and along the way teach, inspire, promote, learn from others with PingPongPhysio.

I'm highly energetic and motivated person and I love to work as a member or a leader of a team. I especially enjoy working with changing projects and taking them forward with rapid pace but high precision. I am talkative and sociable but also known for getting "in the zone" while concentrating on detailed work like programming or designing.
