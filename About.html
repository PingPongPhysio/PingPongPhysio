Background

I was fortunate to grow up with the support of multiple coaches who cared deeply about my table tennis development and personal growth. They all have positively impacted my values and skills to this day. With them, I began representing England at the age of 11 and reached Number 1 in all age categories under 21 in England. Their expertise has enhanced my understanding of many diverse ways of teaching table tennis. Through this experience, I have developed several teaching methods tailored to different types of players' learning styles and skill levels.

Athlete, Physiotherapist and Coach

At the age of 16, I moved to Austria to train as a full-time table tennis athlete at Linz AG Froschberg Club for 2 years. That year, I was selected to compete in the 2018 Gold Coast Commonwealth Games in Australia, where our team won a bronze medal in the event. I continued competing for England and playing in the league abroad in Spain while studying Sports Rehabilitation (BSc Hons) at the University of Nottingham. Afterwards, I decided to further enhance my skills by completing a 2 year Physiotherapy Masters (MSc Pre-registration) at St George's University of London while coaching at my home club, Ellenborough Table Tennis Club, as well as at Woodford School.

Change in Nationality

My mum was born in Turkey, and my dad was born in Seychelles. I do question why I look very English, and it may be because of the lack of sun or because I am always in the table tennis hall.

During the summer of 2023, three lovely table tennis friends and I attended a table tennis training camp in Seychelles, and I had a chance to visit my wonderful family, some for the first time. I really connected with the people, culture and atmosphere. I especially felt valued in my table tennis and as a person in the association. I am beyond grateful to have represented the country I was born in, and I am beyond excited to represent the country where my family is from. Therefore, I have changed my table tennis nationality to Seychelles. Currently, I have moved to the Milton Keynes area, and my dream is to see how far I can take my table tennis career and, along the way, teach, inspire, promote, and learn from others with PingPongPhysio.
