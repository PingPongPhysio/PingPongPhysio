My table tennis journey

I have had multiple coaches growing up and have learnt many diverse aspects of table tennis from each one. Through this experience, I have developed multiple teaching methods tailored to the type of player. Throughout my table tennis career, I was extremely fortunate to have input from multiple coaches between the ages of 7 and 17, who all provided care and support for my table tennis and personal development. They have impacted my values and skills to this day.

At the age of 16, I was selected to compete in the 2018 Gold Coast Commonwealth Games in Australia, where our team won a bronze medal in the event. I moved to Austria without my family, to train as a full-time table tennis athlete at Linz AG Froschberg Club for 2.5 years. I continued competing for England and playing in the league abroad in Spain while studying at the University of Nottingham. Afterwards, I decided to further enhance my physiotherapy skills at St George's University of London while coaching at my home club, Ellenborough Table Tennis Club, as well as at Woodford School.

Change of table tennis nationality:

I have decided to change my table tennis nationality to Seychelles. During the summer of 2023, three lovely table tennis friends and I were invited to a table tennis training camp in Seychelles, and I had a chance to visit my amazing family (my dad is from Seychelles) for the first time. I really connected with the people, culture and atmosphere. I felt valued in my table tennis and as a person, and I am beyond excited to represent them in future events. Currently, I have moved to the Milton Keynes area, and my dream is to see how far I can take my table tennis career and, along the way, teach, inspire, promote, and learn from others with PingPongPhysio.

Background

I was fortunate to grow up with the support of multiple coaches who cared deeply about my table tennis development and personal growth. They all have positively impacted my values and skills to this day. With them, I began representing England at the age of 11 and reached Number 1 in all age categories under 21 in England. Their expertise has enhanced my understanding of many diverse ways of teaching table tennis. Through this experience, I have developed several teaching methods tailored to different types of players' learning styles and skill levels.

Athlete, Physiotherapist and Coach

At the age of 16, I moved to Austria to train as a full-time table tennis athlete at Linz AG Froschberg Club for 2 years. That year, I was selected to compete in the 2018 Gold Coast Commonwealth Games in Australia, where our team won a bronze medal in the event. I continued competing for England and playing in the league abroad in Spain while studying Sports Rehabilitation (BSc Hons) at the University of Nottingham. Afterwards, I decided to further enhance my skills by completing a 2 year Physiotherapy Masters (MSc Pre-registration) at St George's University of London while coaching at my home club, Ellenborough Table Tennis Club, as well as at Woodford School.

Change in Nationality

My mum was born in Turkey, and my dad was born in Seychelles. I do question why I look very English, and it may be because of the lack of sun or because I am always in the table tennis hall.

During the summer of 2023, three lovely table tennis friends and I attended a table tennis training camp in Seychelles, and I had a chance to visit my wonderful family, some for the first time. I really connected with the people, culture and atmosphere. I especially felt valued in my table tennis and as a person in the association. I am beyond grateful to have represented the country I was born in, and I am beyond excited to represent the country where my family is from. Therefore, I have changed my table tennis nationality to Seychelles. Currently, I have moved to the Milton Keynes area, and my dream is to see how far I can take my table tennis career and, along the way, teach, inspire, promote, and learn from others with PingPongPhysio.

What Now?

I am continuing my training to compete for Seychelles. I treat multiple patients weekly as a Physiotherapist at the Back and Body Clinic. I coach Table Tennis to help people simplify and grow into better players, no matter their age.

I am currently based in Buckinghamshire, and my goal is to see how far I can take my table tennis career and, along the way, educate, inspire, promote and learn from others with PingPongPhysio.
