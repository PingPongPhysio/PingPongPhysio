About PingPongPhysio

Making Table Tennis More Enjoyable: For Players of All Levels

My main goal is to find multiple strategies to make this sport easier to understand and enjoyable to facilitate long-term physical activity.

Current

I am currently completing my master's in Physiotherapy at St George's University of London and will graduate in July. Throughout my undergraduate degree in Sports Rehabilitation and master's degrees, I have moved towards being very passionate about combining both physiotherapy and table tennis to help promote long-term exercise.

My table tennis journey

I had multiple coaches growing up and learnt many diverse aspects of table tennis through each coach. Luckily, throughout my table tennis career, I had about 5-7 coaches between the ages of 7 and 17 who provided care and support towards my table tennis and personal development. They have impacted my values and skills to this day (thank you to Urfi, Zoli, Antony, Tibi, Mark, Atilla, Peter, Guri and Zsolt). I moved to Austria at the age of 16 alone to train full-time in table tennis at Linz AG Froschberg Club for 2.5 years. I further developed my game with exposure to inspiring top female players. I then moved to attend Nottingham University, where I achieved a First Class Honour in Sports Rehabilitation degree and adapted my table tennis strategies (thank you Kelly). I am currently completing my Master's degree at St George's University of London and have moved back to North London coaching at Ellenborough Table Tennis Club and Woodford Table Tennis Club. Due to not being able to continue financially to compete for England, in 2023, I decided to change my table tennis nationality to Seychelles. My dad is Seychelios and I have had a passport since I was a baby. During the summer of 2023, I went to a training camp in Seychelles and visited my large family for the first time. I really connected with the culture, people and atmosphere. I felt valued in my table tennis and as a person, and I am beyond excited to represent them in future events. I have to wait 5 years until I can represent Seychelles in bigger events due to the African Table Tennis Federation rules. My dream is to see how far I can take my table tennis career and, along the way, teach, inspire, promote, and learn from others with PingPongPhysio.

My table tennis achievements

  • Commonwealth Games 2018 bronze medalist
  • Finishing 2nd in Senior womens ranking before switching nationality to Seychelles
  • Gold in under 11, 12, 13, 15, 18 and 21 Nationals
  • Number 1 in each age catogary from under 11 to under 21's
  • Played in French league N2, Austrian premier league, Bundaslega, European league, Spanish league N2
  • Played for England abroad in individual and team tournaments
